## Tone Options and Characteristics:

### Professional/Formal (Default)
- Standard business letter language
- Respectful but firm
- Objective presentation of facts
- Appropriate for most demand letters

### Assertive/Firm
- Stronger language emphasizing liability
- More direct statements about consequences
- Confident tone about the strength of the case
- Useful when liability is clear and damages are substantial

### Diplomatic/Conciliatory
- Softer language focused on resolution
- Emphasis on mutual benefit of settlement
- Less confrontational phrasing
- Appropriate when maintaining relationship is important

### Urgent/Pressing
- Emphasis on time-sensitivity
- Stronger language about deadlines
- More explicit about next steps if demand unmet
- Appropriate for statute of limitations concerns or pressing damages
